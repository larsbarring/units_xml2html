SI Base Units

The seven fundamental units of the International System of Units (SI)

meterm
SI base unit
The meter is the length of the path travelled by light in vacuum during a time interval of 1/299 792 458 of a second.
kilogramkg
SI base unit
The kilogram is the unit of mass; it is equal to the mass of the international prototype of the kilogram.
seconds
SI base unit
The second is the duration of 9 192 631 770 periods of the radiation corresponding to the transition between the two hyperfine levels of the ground state of the cesium 133 atom.
ampereA
SI base unit
The ampere is that constant current which, if maintained in two straight parallel conductors of infinite length, of negligible circular cross-section, and placed 1 meter apart in vacuum, would produce between these conductors a force equal to 2e-7 newton per meter of length.
kelvinK
SI base unit
The kelvin, unit of thermodynamic temperature, is the fraction 1/273.16 of the thermodynamic temperature of the triple point of water.
molemol
SI base unit
The mole is the amount of substance of a system which contains as many elementary entities as there are atoms in 0.012 kilogram of carbon 12. When the mole is used, the elementary entities must be specified and may be atoms, molecules, ions, electrons, other particles, or specified groups of such particles.
candelacd
SI base unit
The candela is the luminous intensity, in a given direction, of a source that emits monochromatic radiation of frequency 540e12 hertz and that has a radiant intensity in that direction of 1/683 watt per steradian.

SI Derived Units

radianrad
SI derived unit
Standard unit of angular measure, an angle that creates an arc in a circle equal in length to that circle's radius (roughly 57.3 degrees); as a ratio of two lengths, it has no dimension
steradiansr Derived from: rad^2
SI derived unit
Standard unit of solid angle measure, it is the solid angle which cuts out an area on a sphere that is the square of the sphere's radius; as a ratio of two areas, it has no dimension
hertzHz Derived from: 1/s
SI derived unit
Unit of frequency meaning one cycle per second
gramg Derived from: 1e-3 kg
SI derived unit
Unit of mass equal to one thousandth of a kilogram
newtonN Derived from: m.kg/s^2
SI derived unit
Unit of force; the amount needed to accelerate 1 kilogram of mass at the rate of 1 metre per second squared
pascalPa Derived from: N/m^2
SI derived unit
Unit of pressure (force per unit area) equal to one newton per square meter
jouleJ Derived from: N.m
SI derived unit
Standard unit of work or energy, equal to the work done by a force of one newton acting along a distance of one meter
wattW Derived from: J/s
SI derived unit
Standard unit of power, equivalent to one joule per second, and equal to one ampere flowing across a potential difference of one volt
coulombC Derived from: s.A
SI derived unit
Standard unit of electric charge, the quantity of electricity transported in one second by a current of one ampere
voltV Derived from: W/A
SI derived unit
Standard unit of electric potential difference (and electromotive force); the difference of potential between two points of a conductor carrying a constant current of one ampere and dissipating one watt of power
faradF Derived from: C/V
SI derived unit
Standard unit of capacitance; the amount which, when a capacitor is charged to a potential difference of one volt, carries a charge of one coulomb
ohmΩ Derived from: V/A
SI derived unit
Standard unit of electrical resistance; the resistance between two points of a conductor when a constant potential difference of 1.0 volt, applied to these points, produces in the conductor a current of 1.0 ampere
siemensS Derived from: A/V
SI derived unit
Unit of electric conductance; the reciprocal of one ohm
weberWb Derived from: V.s
SI derived unit
Unit of magnetic flux (product of the average magnetic field times the perpendicular area that it penetrates), expressed as volt-seconds
teslaT Derived from: Wb/m^2
SI derived unit
Unit of flux density, equal to one weber per square meter
henryH Derived from: Wb/A
SI derived unit
Unit of inductance; where a circuit's current changes at a constant rate of 1 ampere per second, results in a generation of 1 V of potential difference
degree_Celsius (plural: degrees_Celsius ) °C Derived from: K @ 273.15
SI derived unit
Unit (and scale) of temperature, with same magnitude as the kelvin and a zero-point offset of 273.15
lumenlm Derived from: cd.sr
SI derived unit
Unit of luminous flux, a measure of the total "amount" of visible light emitted by a source; one candela-steradian
luxlx Derived from: lm/m^2
SI derived unit
Unit of illuminance and luminous emittance, measuring luminous flux per unit area, and used as a measure of intensity of light; equal to one lumen per square meter
katalkat Derived from: mol/s
SI derived unit
Unit of catalytic activity (property of a catalyst, such as an enzyme); expresses the ability to break 1 mole of bonds per second under specified conditions

SI Derived Units with Special Names

SI derived units with special names/symbols admitted for reasons of safeguarding human health

becquerelBq Derived from: 1/s
SI derived unit with special names/symbols admitted for reasons of safeguarding human health
Unit of radioactivity; the activity of a quantity of material in which one nucleus decays per second (hence, equivalent to one unit per second)
grayGy Derived from: J/kg
SI derived unit with special names/symbols admitted for reasons of safeguarding human health
Unit of ionizing radiation, a measure of the absorbed dose of radiation; absorption of one joule of radiation energy by one kilogram of matter
sievertSv Derived from: J/kg
SI derived unit with special names/symbols admitted for reasons of safeguarding human health
Unit of ionizing radiation dose, measuring the health effect of low levels of ionizing radiation on the human body
